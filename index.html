Why Repairing Glass Quickly Matters

Prompt glass repair protects your property from weather damage, improves energy efficiency, and enhances security. Delaying repairs can lead to bigger problems, including water intrusion and reduced insulation.

Common Glass Issues in Ottawa Homes

  • Cracks and Chips – Caused by impact or temperature changes; early repair prevents further damage.
  • Foggy or Cloudy Glass – Often from seal failures in double-pane windows, reducing clarity and insulation.
  • Broken Panes – Resulting from accidents or storms, requiring immediate attention.
  • Scratches and Surface Damage – Usually from cleaning mishaps or wear over time.

When to Repair vs. Replace Glass

Small cracks and chips can usually be repaired quickly and affordably. However, when the glass is shattered, severely cracked, or insulation seals fail, full replacement offers a safer and more durable solution.

Key tip: Address minor damage immediately to avoid costly replacements later.

Popular Glass Repair Methods

  1. Resin Injection — Fills cracks and chips to restore strength and appearance.
  2. Glass Patch Repair — Temporary fix using adhesive patches for small breaks.
  3. Pane Replacement — Removing and replacing broken or foggy glass panels.
  4. Seal Repair — Restoring or replacing seals on double-pane units to fix fogging.

How to Choose the Right Glass Repair Company in Ottawa

Look for certified professionals with local experience, strong customer reviews, insurance, and transparent pricing. Emergency services and quick response times can be a big plus.

Residential vs. Commercial Repairs

Residential repairs often focus on single or double-pane windows, while commercial glass repair may involve larger panels, storefront windows, or security glass. Verify that your contractor has the proper experience for your type of property.

Maintaining Glass After Repair

  • Use gentle, non-abrasive cleaning products.
  • Inspect for new damage regularly.
  • Keep repair documentation for warranty and future reference.
  • Avoid harsh impacts or heavy loads on repaired glass.
